“Great Media Center PC”
PostedApril 30, 2015
Name: DLR1
Location:Hernando, FL, USA
This review is also on Amazon with a few additions.
I just purchased this "mini" pc from HP and configured it as a media PC using KODI XBMC. The pros are: small size and weight, 7200 RPM drive, and good price for what it does. The cons and why I only gave 4 stars are: it has 4 usb3 ports but one is needed for a Bluetooth dongle specs say Bluetooth is "integrated"and it's NOT!, directions are non existent, the wireless keyboard has no on/off switch so you can't tell how long batteries will last, there is no visible disk drive indicator, and finally I attempted to contact HP on their support line asking about the non existent integrated Bluetooth and finally hung up after waiting a half hour on hold. I did post this as a question on their "support page" and we'll see if that brings some intelligent answer.
This IS a great PC but a few instructions would be helpful including how to remove batteries from the keyboard. I finally figured that out by exerting a great amount of force outward.
Update 2/24/15
I have purchased the 4GB upgrade and installed it. This mini now has much more power and speed than my large Gateway workstation. Upgrade cost $31.99, involved removing 7 screws, and plugging in the new memory took10 minutes work. I have been monitoring the heat from this small unit and it is mildly warm after running many hours. I thought about adding a solid state hard drive since it has another slot, believe it or not and we'll see in the future. It boots up to the desktop in 28 seconds after removing the HP bloat ware. I also have discovered that this unit DOES have integrated Bluetooth and I assumed wrong originally. The keyboard/mouse combo included is in fact a wireless non Bluetooth keyboard with a dongle that requires you to use one of the four USB ports or purchase a Bluetooth version which will not need a port. I am leaving the four stars as I don't believe that HP is too responsive to user queries.
I believe an icon should appear on the PC that gives the full instructions and a card informing you of this! I have found that the Wi_Fi works poorly and in fact is the worst WiFi of all devices I own. Contacting HP for the simplest question is frustrating and after being told that the wait time was about 10 minutes and actually waiting for 30 minutes, I hung up and went it alone. Support is poor in my opinion , should have the additional memory 8 Gig or at least an option for the upgrade. I added my own for $31 more and am considering getting rid of the WiFi card and purchasing a good one.
